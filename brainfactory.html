    What is BrainFactory?

    BrainFactory is a one-day event with highly interactive, immersive, and inspiring hands-on workshops, brainstorming sessions, and protoyping exercises.

    For 2025, we're reimagining BrainFactory into interactive workshops where you not only get to experience legal design, you get to dive into a specific area of legal design. Our BrainFactory team together with our Partners are building workshops that arise from real life examples from the cross-sections of law and design thinking.

    Brains on for a full day of legal design! You'll attend two workshops - one in the morning and one in the afternoon - where you'll get to know other interested and like-minded legal professionals and dive into the topics at hand. Are you new to legal design? Don't worry - we've got you covered. We have a specific workshop track to get your feet wet in legal design and still pick up more tips and tricks if you have some experience.

    Program

    Locations: In and around central Helsinki (walking distance from the Summit venue)

    9:00 - 13:00

    Morning Workshops

    Can AI Agents Master Legal Design?

    In this hands-on session, you’ll build your own AI “legal design team,” run a full legal design sprint, and compare results in real time. You'll both think and experiment how both the legal design process and roles could convert into prompts and guidance for AI. See how multi-agent collaboration could reshape legal work.

    Facilitators:

    • Antti Innanen, CEO and Founder, Dot. Legal Design
    • Marco Imperiale, Founder and Managing Director, Better Ipsum
    • Cat Moon, Law Professor and Founding Co-Director, Vanderbilt AI + Law Lab (VAILL), Vanderbilt Law School

    From Insights to Action: Advancing People-Centred Justice through Legal Design

    What if legal systems were designed like great services—intuitive, human, and radically focused on the people they serve? In this dynamic, hands-on workshop, we’ll roll up our sleeves and explore how legal design can move us from understanding justice needs to delivering people-centred solutions. Hosted by HiiL – The Hague Institute for Innovation of Law, this session brings to life insights from the Justice Innovation Lab’s work in Tunisia, Nigeria, Burkina Faso, Ethiopia, and Niger. You’ll hear powerful, real-world stories from legal designers and justice innovators on the ground—and then become part of the story. Together, we’ll step into the shoes of justice users, identify urgent legal challenges, and co-create breakthrough ideas using design tools. Expect fast-paced collaboration, fresh thinking, and a few unexpected twists along the way. Whether you’re a policymaker, practitioner, designer, or curious changemaker, you’ll leave with practical frameworks and renewed energy for making justice systems truly work for people.

    Facilitators:

    • Zainab Malik, Senior Policy and Advocacy Advisor - HiiL;
    • Viviana Brun, Programme Manager, Justice Innovation Labs - HiiL
    • Andrew Solomon, Lead Justice Collaborator - Rule of Law IDEAS Co-lab
    • Suparat Fern Swatemal, Global Engagement Lead, Thailand Institute of Justice

    Legal Design Writing: Telling the World and Getting Heard

    Do you have something you want to tell the world, but publishing your ideas feels a bit intimidating? Writing is a bit like a monster with razor-sharp deadlines, intimidating reviewer fangs, surrounded by the paralyzing fog of perfectionism? Join this four-hour workshop to tame the publication beast, tell the world and make your ideas heard. Walk away with a roadmap for your work, as well as some key accountability partners.

    Facilitators:

    • Michael Doherty, Professor of Legal Design, Lancaster University
    • Helena Haapio, Contract Strategist, Lexpert Ltd
    • Lisa Toohey, Professor, UNSW Law & Justice
    • Michael Blasie, Dean and Professor of Law, KIMEP University

    Legal Design From First Principles

    Think design is just for creatives? Think again. This science-led workshop draws on human factors, psychology, and usability research to show how lawyers can use proven design principles to make contracts work better for everyone. We’ll strip legal design back to its fundamentals, understanding how people read, think, decide, and act, and use that understanding to build smarter, more user-friendly legal documents. Through short exercises and real-world examples, you’ll explore why good design isn’t about pretty fonts or graphics, it’s about using friction effectively, improving comprehension, and helping people make better decisions. Whether you're new to legal design or looking to strengthen your approach, you'll leave with practical tools grounded in science, not just style.

    Facilitators:

    • Peter Hornsby, Designer - Edgerton Riley

    Organizational Design for Legal Teams in the Age of Artificial Intelligence

    This interactive workshop explores how artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping the principles of organisational design. Participants will dive into practical strategies for building more dynamic and adaptive legal teams — rethinking processes, roles, structures, and culture to stay competitive in a rapidly changing, AI-driven world. By the end of the session, attendees will have actionable tools to rethink organisational design in the context of AI — from redefining job roles to fostering an innovation-ready culture in a legal team.

      Facilitators:

    • Heikki Ilvessalo, CEO, ILVES
    • Maria Sorsimo, Head of Finland, Legora

    Afternoon Workshops

    14:00 – 18:00

    Designing the Future of Law: AI-Powered Legal Design Sprint

    How might we harness AI to accelerate the adoption of legal design across the profession? Join us for an intensive 4-hour design sprint where legal professionals, technologists, and designers will co-create innovative solutions to pressing legal challenges. Participants will collaborate at themed tables focused on high-impact areas like access to justice, contract simplification, and legal visualisation. Guided by the proven BrainFactory × DLA methodology and inspired by lightning talks from legal design pioneers and AI-providers, you’ll ideate, prototype, and pitch ideas that show how AI can make legal systems more human-centered, efficient, and accessible. With support from Newcode.ai, you’ll leave with tangible prototypes - and a deeper understanding of the ROI of integrating AI into legal workflows. Whether you're a lawyer, a designer, or a changemaker in between, this is your chance to shape the future of law.

    Facilitators:

    • Riyal Patel, Francesca Oprandi, Deborah Paracchini and Enila Elezi, DLA Piper
    • Maged Helmy, Newcode.ai

    Contract design: practical techniques to transform your B2B contracts straight away

    In this interactive workshop, contract drafters and negotiators will learn how to transform business contracts to dramatically reduce friction and negotiation times – and improve commercial relationships.We will share our expertise in simplifying complex contracts, focusing on foundational techniques that can get you immediate results.

    Facilitators:

    • Stefania Passera, Contract Design Expert, CEO, Passera Design

    Enabling Digital Transformation through Legal Design

    How might we leverage legal design to create successful digital transformation projects within legal teams and workflows? In this interactive workshop we will present a case, cocreating roadmaps and possible solutions. We will use methods like interviews, empathy maps and idea generation techniques and empower participants. Our guests will leave this workshop with real life examples and skills to execute successful legal transformation.

    Facilitators:

    • Carol Hannud, Legal Transformation, Dispute System Design, and Legal Design Senior Executive, EY and Professor at Fundação Getulio Vargas Law School (Brazil)
    • Pia Madissoo, Legal Transform & Operate Lead, EY (Finland)

    Legal Design for Sustainability

    This workshop explores how legal design can be applied to enhance sustainability-related processes and language. Through hands-on activities and case studies, participants will: 1. co-create clearer sustainability clauses, 2. rethink legal transformation toolkits, and 3. design meaningful stakeholder engagement strategies.

    Facilitators:

    • Sampo Mielityinen, Head of Master’s Programme on Legal Design and Legal Expertise, Laurea University of Applied Sciences
    • Seita Vesa, Professor of Environmental Law, UEF Law School, Research Professor, Finnish Environmental Institute (SYKE)
    • Ebru Metin, Researcher, TalTech, Founder of Legal Design Turkey
    • Anne Ketola, Senior Researcher, Tampere University
